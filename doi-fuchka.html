Doi Fuchka / Dahi Puri Chaat

By Arpita © 2016 Spicy World
Nov 12, 2018
Star Rating WIDGET PACK

Doi Fuchka or Dahi Puri Chaat is one delicious and popular variety from Chaat Gharana. You can get several types of chaat in India from street vendors. They all are very very tasty. In Kolkata, one of the famous chaat is 'doi fuchka'. The hollow chips are filled with spicy potato filling, thick sweet and sour yogurt mixture, sweet tamarind chutney along with lots of fresh coriander leaves and few other ingredients. I have never made Chaat at home while staying in India because you can get them almost everywhere. But now that I am staying in USA, whenever I crave for Chaat, I had to make them at home. Trust me, after following this recipe you will achieve the exact same street style taste and flavors. Enjoy the video with detailed recipe.


Ingredients

  • 20 fuchkas / puris.
  • 1 big, boiled and peeled potato.
  • A cup of boiled yellow peas (motor) and black chana (chola).
  • Half of a onion, finely chopped.
  • 2 green chilies. chopped.
  • A handful of coriander leaves, chopped.
  • Foe bhaja moshla (1 Tablespoon of cumin seeds, 2 Tablespoons of coriander seeds, 2 dry red chilies).
  • 2 Tablespoons of chaat masala.
  • 3 Teaspoons of lemon juice.
  • Salt and sugar.
  • Half cup of yogurt.
  • Water.
  • 1/4th cup of sweet tamarind chutney. (I used store bought one)
  • A handful of sev / jhuri bhaja.






Steps

Dry roast the cumin seeds, coriander seeds and dry red chilies for 2-3 minutes on low flame in a pan.
Then gring them to a fine powder. This is called bhaja moshla. It is a must for Kolkata style fuchka.
In a bowl add boiled and lightly mashed potatoes, boiled peas and chana, some chopped onion, salt, bhaja moshla, chaat masala, half of the coriander leaves and lemon juice. Mix everything well with your hand and check the seasoning.
Beat the yogurt well, then add some salt, 2 Teaspoons of sugar and 3-4 Tablespoons of water. Mix well with a spoon.
Now make holes in the center of the fuchkas or puris.
Fill them with the spicy potato filling, then add chopped raw onion and green chilies.
Next add some more boiled peas and black chana.
Then sprinkle some more bhaja moshla and chaat masala.
After that, add 1-2 Tablespoons of yogurt mixture in each fuchka.
Then add 1 Teaspoon of sweet tamarind chutney in each of them.
Lastly sprinkle some sev and the remaining chopped coriander leaves all over them.

Additional Info

Recipe Category: Appetizers: Vegetarian
Recipe Cuisine: Indian
Preparation Time: 30 minutes
Cooking Time: 20 minutes
Serves: 3

Your Doi fuchka is ready to serve.
  • Serve this immediately ...
